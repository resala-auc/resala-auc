The Step of Support

Mothers Day Director

Helps mothers understand and support children's growth so impact continues beyond the session.

Apply for this role Compare all roles
Best fit signal You can build sessions that help mothers support their children with the resources they have.
Why choose it

If we want to support children, we also need to support the environment around them. Mothers Day helps mothers understand what the children are learning and how they can support them with the resources they have.

Guiding question

If Children Day plants the seed of change, can you help mothers understand it, support it, and keep it growing beyond the session?

Interview task direction

Plan a Mothers Day session series: alignment with Children Day, topics, speakers, respectful activities, and practical takeaways for mothers.

Role requirements

Read this as a checklist

Leadership requirement

Lead a support program that respects mothers and aligns with Children Day, speakers, professors, and the realities families face.

Ownership requirement

Own the bridge between session and home. Make sure the impact can continue beyond what children experience on Saturday.

Skills requirement

  • Respectful communication.
  • Planning and coordination.
  • Ability to contact professors or speakers.
  • Presentation skills.
  • Sensitivity and self-awareness.
  • Ability to align different goals together.
  • Understanding of children's growth and family support.
  • Consistency in bi-weekly planning.

Preferred experiences or passions

  • You care about supporting children through their environment.
  • You can communicate respectfully with mothers and specialists.
  • You are interested in practical family support.

Actual work

What your week-to-week responsibility can include

  1. 01

    Understand Children Day goals.

  2. 02

    Build Mothers Day goals that align with them.

  3. 03

    Plan bi-weekly sessions for mothers.

  4. 04

    Coordinate with professors, speakers, or specialists.

  5. 05

    Validate session topics and materials with trusted guidance.

  6. 06

    Make sure the sessions are practical and respectful.

  7. 07

    Help mothers understand how to support their children.

  8. 08

    Keep Mothers Day and Children Day connected.
